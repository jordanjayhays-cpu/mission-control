One-Page Cheat Sheet (Print This)

Design
Look at Profit column next to each option. Check the box with the HIGHEST profit for Model A. Do the same for Model B.
Forecasting
Add up all 6 forecaster numbers. Divide by 6. That's your average. Multiply by 8 (8 selling months: May–Dec). Enter it.
Production
Model A → FarFarAway (cheapest/slow). Model B → PrettyClose (expensive/fast). Check capacity isn't exceeded.
Boardroom
Always explain WHY. "We picked the option with the highest profit per unit." "We used our own average not Consensus." "We matched suppliers to demand profiles."
Newsvendor
Model B: overage $136.50 > underage $90 → UNDERPRODUCE. When in doubt on Model B, make less than the forecast.
Sales Window
May–December only = 8 months. Zero demand January–April. Always multiply by 8.
