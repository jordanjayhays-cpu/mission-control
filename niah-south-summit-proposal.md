## 1. What Is Niah

AI-driven networking tool for conference after-parties. Instead of random mingling, Niah matches attendees based on:
- Role / industry / goals
- Who they want to meet and why
- Conversation topics they care about

**The metaphor:** Like a skilled host who introduces the right people at a dinner party — but at scale, with 20,000 attendees.

Niah doesn't require an app download. It works via:
- **Email survey** (3 questions, 60 seconds to complete)
- **Web-based matching** (personal link sent to matched pairs before the event)
- **Day-of SMS** (your matched conversation partner is at the bar, come say hi)

## 3. The Problem Niah Solves

At South Summit-scale events, networking is broken:
- **Information overload** — who should you talk to? Everyone looks equally interesting
- **Random collisions** — you meet people but not the *right* people
- **No follow-up** — even if you connect, you lose their contact in the noise
- **Introverts lose** — the loudest networkers dominate; the best connections never happen
- **After-party chaos** — 500+ people in one room, no structure, same 5 conversations

Niah's after-party matchmaking addresses all of these.

## 4. How It Works — Step by Step

### Phase 1: Pre-Event (June 1–4)

**Step 1 — Email survey to all registered attendees**
- Tool: Brevo email platform
- What: 3-question profiling survey (role, who to meet, conversation goals)
- Timing: Send Day 1 (June 1), Day 2 reminder (June 2), Day 3 reminder (June 3)
- Subject line options: "Before the Social Summit — 60 seconds to get matched" / "Skip the small talk at tonight's after-party"

**Step 2 — AI matching algorithm**
- Input: Survey responses
- Output: Matched pairs with compatibility score + reasoning
- Each person gets: "You're matched with [Name] — here's why you should talk: [2-3 sentence reasoning]"
- Matching criteria: role complementarity, goal alignment, industry cross-pollination

**Step 3 — Send match notifications (pre-event)**
- Timing: June 4, morning (before 7:30 PM event)
- Channel: Email + SMS backup
- Content: "Your Social Summit match is waiting. [Name], [Title] at [Org]. Here's why you should meet: [reasoning]. Find them at the Niah corner at the Social Summit bar."

### Phase 2: Day-Of (June 4, 7:30 PM onward)

**Step 4 — Physical presence at La Nave**
- Setup: Niah branded corner/area in the after-party space
- Staff: 1-2 people with tablets to help late sign-ups and troubleshoot
- Materials: QR code for late survey completions, printed match cards for those without phones

**Step 5 — Match notifications as they happen**
- For people who complete the survey during the after-party:
- Real-time matching → SMS within 5 minutes → "Your match is at the bar. Go say hi."

**Step 6 — Post-event follow-up**
- Next day (June 5): Email each matched pair with their connection's contact info
- Prompt: "Continue the conversation — here's how to reach them"
- Also: satisfaction survey (1 question: "Did your match lead to a useful connection?")

## 9. Matching Algorithm — How It Works (Simple Version)

No complex AI needed. A simple weighted scoring system works:

**Profile data from survey:**
1. Role type: Investor / Founder / Corporate / Media / Other
2. Industry: Fintech / Healthtech / AI / Retail / etc.
3. Goal: Find investors / find co-founders / find clients / find partners
4. Who they want to meet: [free text]

**Matching rules (priority order):**
1. **Role complementarity** — founder + investor = high value match
2. **Industry overlap** — same industry + different company = useful
3. **Goal alignment** — one person wants to find X, another person is X
4. **Country diversity** — cross-country connections are more valuable

**Format of a match notification:**
```
You're matched with Ana García, CFO at Banco Santander.

Why: You're a fintech founder looking for corporate partnerships. Ana is a corporate innovation lead at a major Spanish bank actively looking to meet fintech founders.

Where to find her: She'll be at the Niah corner near the main bar around 8 PM.

Say hi: "Hi Ana — I'm matched with you. I'm building [X] and I think you're the person who can help us [goal]."
```
