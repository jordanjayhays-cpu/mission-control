Why Therapy Isn't Helping (Yet)

Standard CBT-based therapy treats the content of your thoughts (change the thought, change the feeling). But your problem is deeper — it's a frame problem. The entire way you're processing reality has a structural crack in it. CBT can't fix a structural crack with behavioral techniques.

What will actually help:

• ACT (Acceptance & Commitment Therapy) — Teaches you to hold the existential thoughts without fighting them, then act from your values anyway. Designed exactly for your territory.

• DBT (Dialectical Behavior Therapy) — Highly structured, teaches crisis tolerance and emotional regulation. You have a crisis tolerance problem (the alarm going off all day).

• IFS (Internal Family Systems) — Treats your psyche as a system of "parts." You sound like you have a lot of exiled parts that need to be heard, not destroyed.

• Psilocybin-assisted therapy — Emerging, showing dramatic results for treatment-resistant existential depression. Not philosophy. Neurobiology. Breaking the rigid pattern.

The Daily Practice Framework

Start here. Just this. Nothing else today.

Morning: 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding (5 minutes) Name 5 things you see → 4 you hear → 3 you can touch → 2 you smell → 1 you taste. This is not philosophy. This is your nervous system. You're stuck in your prefrontal cortex (overthinking). This pulls you back into your body.
During the spiral: The Paradox Interrupt When you notice the loop starting — "nothing matters so why try" — pause and say out loud: "I notice I'm in the paradox loop." Just naming it creates a gap. The gap is all you need right now. You don't need to solve it.
When you feel "unhuman" / depersonalized Press your palms firmly together for 30 seconds. Feel the texture, warmth, pressure. Or hold ice in your hand. Physical sensation interrupts the derealization loop. Your nervous system needs a hard anchor, not a philosophical one.
Evening: Write one sentence Not a journal. Not analysis. One sentence: "Today I noticed ___." That's it. It trains your brain to notice participatory knowing (Vervaeke's term) — the direct relationship with reality, not the thought about reality.

The Single Most Important Insight

Your problem is participatory knowing — the fourth kind of knowing that Vervaeke describes. You know things about reality (propositional), you know how to do things (procedural), you know what angle to see things from (perspectival).
