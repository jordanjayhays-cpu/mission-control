# Madrid Neighborhood Analysis — Micro-Launch Selection

---

## Why Chamberí (Recommended First)

**Demographics:**
- Young professionals, 25–40, high disposable income
- Many expats/English speakers (good for early demand if you want to test English-language version)
- Quiet enough that people actually live there (not tourists)

**Business density:**
- Independent shops, cafes, small businesses — not chains
- Has a "neighborhood feel" that makes it credible for independent therapists
- Google Maps: search "masaje Chamberí Madrid" → 15+ results, many solo practitioners

**Metro access:**
- Line 1: Bilbao, Tribunal, Gran Vía (transitional)
- Line 2: Quevedo, Canal, San Bernardo (mostly western edge)
- Line 7: Bilbao, Almagro (eastern edge)
- Key spots: Rubén Darío (north), Bilbao (south) — 10-min walk means good coverage

**Risk:** It's a gentrifying area — rents are rising. Therapists might not be able to afford to stay long-term.

---

## Why Salamanca (Second Option)

**Demographics:**
- Wealthier, older, established professionals
- Higher average spend per session (€60–100 range is expected)
- More traditional — less likely to try a new platform

**Business density:**
- Wealthy residential + business district (Calle Serrano)
- More studios than solo practitioners
- "Salamanca" = 4-star hotels, high-end wellness

**Metro access:**
- Line 4: Serrano, Goya, Velázquez
- Line 2: Goya, Manuel Becerra
- Line 6: Manuel Becerra, O'Donnell
- Very well connected

**Risk:** Higher expectations, less price-sensitive but harder to get first meeting (gatekeepers, receptionist, booking systems already in place)

---

## Why NOT Malasaña or Chueca (Yet)

**Malasaña:**
- Great vibe, but younger/more casual — €20–30 massage is the expectation, not €60+
- Too much foot traffic, too many tourists — hard to build consistent client base
- Many independent businesses but the culture is "cheap and quick"

**Chueca:**
- Similar to Malasaña — trendy, younger demographic
- LGBTQ+ community is health/wellness oriented but niche
- Could be a second neighborhood after Salamanca

**Centro (Sol, Gran Vía):**
- Tourists. No loyalty, no repeat. Not the right market for a platform that needs repeat bookings to prove retention.

## How to Pick the Exact 5 Studios

**Filter in Google Maps:**
1. Search: "masaje Madrid Chamberí"
2. Filter: 4+ stars, not a chain
3. Look for: solo practitioner profile (not "Zen Massage Center")
4. Check: do they have a booking link? If they have Booksy, that's not a dealbreaker but note it
5. Walk in. Literally walk in. Introduce yourself. The best clients come from physical presence.

**Don't overthink the list.** You're looking for 5 within 1km radius of each other. Start with the ones that have the most accessible Google profiles (recent reviews, real photos, specific service menu). Those are the ones who care about their presence.
