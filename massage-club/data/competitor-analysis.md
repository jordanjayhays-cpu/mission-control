# Competitor Analysis — Massage Pass Positioning

---

## The Landscape

| Competitor | Target | Model | Weakness for Us |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Booksy** | Studios, chains, established practices | €29–99/month subscription | Too expensive for solo therapists starting out |
| **Treatwell** | Studios + day spas | Commission-based (10–15%) + subscription | Heavy on spa/wellness, not independent therapists |
| **Fulero** | High-end / wellness | Membership + credits | €49/month minimum — not casual |
| **Google Maps** | Everyone | Listings only | No booking, no trust signal |
| **Instagram/TikTok DMs** | Casual, word-of-mouth | None | No transaction, no accountability |

---

## The Gap We're Filling

**Booksy's core market:** Studios with 5+ therapists, €500+ revenue per day, established client base.

**Our core market:** Solo independent therapist with 4 empty slots per week, 0–2 new clients per month, paying €29/month to Booksy for a listing they barely appear in.

**The Booksy problem:** On Booksy, you're competing with every other therapist on the platform. You show up in search. You're a thumbnail. You're a commodity.

**Massage Pass position:** We only list independent therapists. We only operate in one neighborhood at a time. When you search on Massage Pass, you get 5 therapists — all independent, all nearby, all with real profiles. Not 500 results. Not a scrolling list of strangers. A real shortlist.

---

## How to Position Against Each

### vs. Booksy
Don't say "we're cheaper than Booksy." Say: "Booksy is for studios with full calendars. We're for therapists building theirs from scratch. Free first month, then only 15% when you actually earn something."

### vs. Google Maps
"Google tells you where the massage is. We tell you who's available now and books it in one click."

### vs. Walking In
"We do the same thing as walking in — except you know they have availability before you go, you get a confirmation, and you can read reviews from real clients first."

---

## The Messaging Don'ts

1. **Don't say "app"** — people hear "another app to download and forget about"
2. **Don't say "platform"** — say "we connect you with therapists near you"
3. **Don't compare on price alone** — compare on simplicity and fit
4. **Don't use the word "marketplace"** anywhere — it sounds like you're Airbnb for massages
