Paradromics — VP Marketing & Brand
What it actually is: This is a founder-level narrative job. Matt Angle (CEO) just completed the first human BCI implant (June 2025). The company is about to raise a landmark round. They're not looking for a product marketer — they're looking for someone who can make Paradromics the Neuralink alternative that institutional VCs and the public believe in. The narrative has to work on patients, investors, regulators, and the scientific community simultaneously.
Participation pointer: Before applying, read Matt Angle's 2024 Nature paper on the neural interface architecture. Come with a one-pager: "Three narratives Paradromics should own that Neuralink can't." That's your interview opener. The VP who walks in knowing the science and the story wins. Follow the company on LinkedIn — Matt Angle posts frequently about BCI milestones. Reference a specific post in your outreach.
MBA edge: You don't need a neuroscience degree — you need to show you can translate "high-density neural recording at 192 channels" into a sentence a pension fund will invest in. Frame it as category creation, not marketing.
Neuralink — Product Operations
What it actually is: Non-technical project coordinator role at the world's most watched BCI company. You'll be the connective tissue between the engineering team (building the N1 chip) and the clinical team (running the PRIME study). Think: timeline management, cross-functional sync, risk tracking, vendor coordination. It's a $68K–$114K ops role — not glamorous, but近距离 the science.
Participation pointer: Study Neuralink's FDA approval journey — the FDA rejected them twice before approving the human trial. Know the specific failure points (N1 chip biocompatibility, animal welfare concerns). Ask during interview: "What was the hardest operational lesson from the FDA rejection and how would you structure the ops process differently?" Shows you did your homework.
MBA edge: MBA gives you the systems-thinking lens: how does a project this complex not fall apart? Frame your experience with multi-stakeholder coordination, not just project management terminology.
